## 1. Combination Problem

### Objection

Even if micro-experiences exist at the fundamental level, we cannot explain how they combine into the **unified macro-experience** of a single subject. Micro-experiences seem to remain separate; summing them does not yield "what it is like" to be me.

**Sources**: Coleman (2014), James (1890), Goff (2019) Ch. 8.

### Response Strategy

1. **Shared problem**: Emergentism faces symmetric "emergence problem"—how does full qualia appear from zero qualia? Panpsychism at least posits experiential precursors.
2. **Fusion** (Seager): Micro-subjects fuse into new subject rather than aggregate; macro-subject is not mere sum.
3. **Cosmopsychism fallback** (Chalmers, Korman): One cosmic subject; individuals are partial views—combination reversed.
4. **IIT formalism**: Integration (Φ) measures irreducible unified cause-effect structure; not subject metaphysics but formal correlate.
5. **Subject-summing with constraints** (Goff): Composition rules restrict illegitimate combinations.

### Status: *Partial*

Combination remains hardest objection. IIT and fusion are promising but incomplete. Cosmopsychism solves combination at cost of explaining locality.

### Empirical Hook

- Measure Φ during perceptual binding (e.g., binocular rivalry, multisensory integration)
- `research/empirical/combination_model.py` — simulate fusion vs. summing
- Anesthesia: integration breakdown correlates with unity loss (P4)

## 2. Causal Exclusion Problem

### Objection

If mental properties are distinct from physical properties, they are causally excluded by complete physical causation (Kim). If identical to physical properties, panpsychism collapses into physicalism. Either way, experiential properties seem epiphenomenal or redundant.

**Sources**: Kim (1998), Papineau.

### Response Strategy

1. **Russellian monism**: Experiential properties are **intrinsic aspect** of same entities physics describes structurally. No overdetermination—one entity, two aspects.
2. **Not dualism**: Not two substances; one substance with structural (physical) and intrinsic (experiential) descriptions.
3. **A5**: Experiential properties participate in causal structure as intrinsic side of causal powers.

### Status: *Partial*

Russellian monism widely debated. Critics argue intrinsic nature still excluded or unknown. Requires accepting underdetermination of physics re: intrinsics.

### Empirical Hook

- No direct test of intrinsic nature
- Anesthesia/causal intervention affects both behavior and (reported) experience consistently—consistent with single causal entity

## 3. Too Many Minds (Absurdity)

### Objection

Panpsychism implies electrons, tables, and thermostats have experiences—absurd proliferation of minds.

**Sources**: Popper, common intuition pumps.

### Response Strategy

1. **Panprotopsychism** (Chalmers 2013): Fundamental entities have **proto-experience**, not full beliefs, desires, or selfhood.
2. **No cognitive chauvinism**: Proto-experience is minimal—perhaps pure "what-it-is-likeness" without content.
3. **Macro vs. micro**: Only highly integrated systems have rich unified experience; ubiquity ≠ equality.

### Status: *Resolved* (for panprotopsychism variant)

Full panpsychism (micro-minds) remains vulnerable; our axioms use panprotopsychism (A3).

### Empirical Hook

- Distinguish Φ (integration) from cognitive access measures
- P5: high Φ in simple systems without consciousness reports challenges IIT, not panprotopsychism per se

## 5. Occam's Razor

### Objection

Panpsychism multiplies entities/experiences unnecessarily. Physicalism is simpler.

**Sources**: Smart, Armstrong tradition.

### Response Strategy

1. **Count kinds, not entities**: Panpsychism posits **one kind** (experiential/intrinsic) vs. physicalism's **two** (dead matter + emergent mind).
2. **Brute emergence cost**: Emergence of qualia from non-qualia is unexplained surplus; panpsychism avoids brute leap.
3. **Parsimony is theory-relative**: Simplest physics ≠ simplest metaphysics.

### Status: *Partial*

Debated. Illusionism may win on parsimony if qualia eliminated. Physicalists deny brute emergence is costly.

### Empirical Hook

- Parsimony analysis in `PANPSYCHISM_RESEARCH.md`
- If G1 (illusionism) succeeds, Occam favors illusionism

## 6. Evolution and Adaptivity

### Objection

Consciousness evolved for fitness; fundamental consciousness would not be adaptive at micro-level. Evolution supports emergentism.

**Sources**: Darwinian critics, Dennett.

### Response Strategy

1. **Fundamental ≠ adaptive**: Proto-experience ubiquitous does not mean each instance selected for function.
2. **Access vs. phenomenal** (Block): Evolution may select cognitive access; phenomenal side may be intrinsic correlate.
3. **Integration selected**: What evolves is integrated cognitive architecture; Φ may correlate without proto-experience being individually adaptive.

### Status: *Partial*

Evolutionary story for panpsychism weaker than for functional accounts. Does not refute ubiquity—correlation vs. function.

### Empirical Hook

- P4: dissociate phenomenal loss from cognitive loss under anesthesia
- Lesion studies: blindsight (access without report)

## 7. Illusionism

### Objection

There are no real phenomenal properties—only introspective illusion that we have qualia. Panpsychism posits what does not exist.

**Sources**: Frankish (2016), Dennett.

### Response Strategy

1. **Phenomenal realism**: First-person experience is data to explain, not eliminate.
2. **Illusionism burden**: Must explain why illusion is so persistent and structured; may smuggle qualia back in via "seeming."
3. **G1 falsifier**: If illusionism fully succeeds, panpsychism refuted—take seriously.

### Status: *Open*

Live debate. Panpsychism and illusionism both respond to hard problem; incompatible on existence of qualia.

### Empirical Hook

- No decisive test; indirect via P1–P6 (if Φ tracks real phenomenology, illusionism weakens)

P5 insufficient data. Biological naturalism remains default for many neuroscientists.

## 10. Epiphenomenalism

### Objection

If experiential properties exist but do not cause behavior, they are explanatorily idle.

**Sources**: Kim, Churchland.

### Response Strategy

1. **A5**: Experiential = intrinsic aspect of causally efficacious entities.
2. **Reject epiphenomenal panpsychism**: Not our variant.
3. **Structure + intrinsic**: Physics describes causal structure; experience is what-it-is-like of that structure from inside.

### Status: *Partial*

Depends on Russellian monism success. Epiphenomenal panpsychism is weaker variant we reject.

### Empirical Hook

- Mental causation assumed in intervention studies; anesthesia affects both

## 11. Small Combination Problem (Subject Unity)

### Objection

Even within human consciousness, unity is partial (split-brain, multi-stable perception). Panpsychist unity claims oversimplify.

**Sources**: Bayne (2010), Coleman.

2. **Split-brain as partial decomposition**: Supports integration model—when integration breaks, unity breaks.
3. **Not all-or-nothing**: Panpsychism compatible with partial unity.

### Status: *Partial*

Actually supports panpsychist integration framing.

## 12. Cosmopsychism and the Fragmentation Problem (BFC Branch)

### Objection

If we adopt cosmopsychism, how do **individual subjects derive** from one cosmic consciousness? This is the **fragmentation problem** (One→many)—the mirror of the combination problem.

**Sources**: Korman, Schneider; BFC branch [`cosmopsychism_axioms.md`](cosmopsychism_axioms.md).

### Response Strategy

1. **Fragmentation / filtering (F2)**: Brains (or integrators) act as localized filters on cosmic field.
2. **MWI branching (F3, speculative)**: Decoherence splits mutually inaccessible experiential contexts.
3. **Indexicality**: "I" indexes one fragment; not all cosmic content is locally accessible.
4. **Collective states (F4)**: Temporary re-coupling at group scale—empirically tractable via P7.

### Status: *Partial*

Fragmentation problem acknowledged; filter model testable at P9; cosmic derivation not directly observable.

### Empirical Hook

- P7 hyperscanning; P9 filter correlates; `fragmentation_model.py`
