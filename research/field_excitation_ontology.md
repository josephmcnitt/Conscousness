# Field Excitation Ontology (FEO)

**Part III** of the Panpsychism Research Program. Unifies Part I (panprotopsychism) and Part II (BFC) under one physical metaphor.

**Thesis (research hypothesis):** The consciousness field is fundamental. Macro-subjects are **localized, stable excitations** in that field—like an electron is a quantum excitation of the electromagnetic field. Brains are **boundary conditions** that stabilize particular modes.

## Core Axioms F6–F8

### F6 — Field Excitation

**Macro-subjects are localized excitations (stable modes) of the consciousness field, not separate substances.**

- "I" is the excitation; the field is what excites
- No combination problem from summing micro-minds—you are a mode, not an aggregate
- **Empirical hook**: P9 filter model; excitation amplitude correlates with reported intensity

### F7 — Mode Spectrum

**The field supports a spectrum of modes; integration (Φ) measures mode coherence and irreducibility of the excitation.**

- Narrow, coherent wavepacket = unified subject (high Φ)
- Broad, noisy wavepacket = filter failure (low Φ, high disorganization)
- **Empirical hook**: P1, P4, P10–P12

### F8 — Creative Perturbation

**Creativity is structured perturbation of the excitation—exploring adjacent modes without filter failure.**

- Flow = edge of stability (F7 + moderate field coupling)
- Insight = phase transition between attractors (P11)
- Psychosis = uncontrolled mode flooding (F5 / P8)
- **Empirical hook**: P10 inverted-U; P12 joint improv entrainment
