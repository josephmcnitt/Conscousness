# MWI and Consciousness: Correlation with Branching Fragmentation Cosmopsychism

Maps Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) of quantum mechanics to the BFC research branch. **Speculative synthesis—not proof of MWI or cosmopsychism.**

---

## Executive Summary

MWI and cosmopsychism address **different problems** that align structurally:

- **MWI**: Why one outcome if quantum mechanics is linear and unitary? Answer: all outcomes occur in branching worlds; no collapse.
- **Cosmopsychism**: How do local subjects arise if experience is fundamental? Answer: fragmentation of cosmic field into partial views.
- **BFC proposal**: **Decoherence-driven branching = fragmentation mechanism**—perspective fixation without collapse.

Nearest empirical work remains **collective neuroscience (P7–P9)**, not direct branch detection.

## Why MWI Helps BFC

1. **No collapse mystery** — consciousness need not cause or require wavefunction collapse (avoids Wigner/von Neumann consciousness-collapse theories MWI rejects)

2. **Natural privacy** — branches are mutually inaccessible; aligns with **boundary between subjects**

3. **Inverts combination** — don't sum electron experiences; explain **why your stream is one branch's history**

4. **Multiscale narrative** — cosmic field → branch → filtered local subject → temporary group coupling → re-filter

5. **Wallace (2012)** — decision-theoretic derivation of MWI probability from rational agency; structure without collapse may extend to experiential indexing (speculative)

## Why MWI Hurts BFC (Objections)

### 1. Experience Multiplication

If every branch is real and experiential, **vast multiplication** of experiences at every quantum event—worse than "too many minds."

**Responses documented in program:**
- **Panprotopsychism at branch level**: proto-experience, not full macro-minds per branch
- **Indexicality**: only the branch indexed by "I" is subjectively accessible; others are real but not experienced by this subject (Vaidman-style)
- **Sparse experientiality**: only high-Φ structures in each branch instantiate rich experience (IIT constraint)

**Status**: Partial—multiplication objection not fully resolved.

### 2. Preferred Basis Problem

Why does fragmentation take **neural/measurement bases** rather than arbitrary superpositions?

**Response strategy:**
- Decoherence selects quasi-classical bases dynamically
- IIT: consciousness tied to integrated cause-effect structure in particular mechanisms
- Filter model: brains are macroscopic decoherence structures—natural basis selectors

**Status**: Open—same problem affects MWI generally.

### 3. No Empirical Access to Other Branches

Cannot visit other branches; cannot **test** their experiential nature directly.

**Program stance:**
- MWI predictions are **structural/interpretive**
- Near-term empirical face = **P7–P9** at neural/social scale, not cross-branch detection

### 4. Albert's Objection

David Albert: MWI may not derive **Born rule** or unique experiential facts without additional structure.

**Program stance:** Document as open; Wallace's program partially addresses probability; experiential indexing separate.

### 5. Illusionism (G1)

If qualia are illusory, entire BFC framework is undermined—applies to whole research program.
