# Literature Review: Panpsychism and Consciousness as Fundamental

Annotated bibliography organized by **argument type**, not author. Each entry includes relevance to the Russellian panprotopsychism research program, key claims, and limitations.

---

## 1. Foundational Philosophy of Mind

### Chalmers, D. (1996). *The Conscious Mind: In Search of a Fundamental Theory*. Oxford University Press.

**Relevance**: Central. Hard problem formulation; panpsychism as serious option.

**Key claims**:
- Phenomenal consciousness is not logically entailed by physical/functional description (explanatory gap).
- "Double-aspect" and panpsychist options avoid brute emergence.
- Combination problem acknowledged as major challenge.

**Limitations**: Does not commit to panpsychism; treats it as one live option. IIT connection developed later.

**Program use**: Primary source for Argument from Hard Problem; sets falsification bar (G4).

---

### Nagel, T. (1979). "Panpsychism." In *Mortal Questions*. Cambridge University Press.

**Relevance**: High. Classic defense of continuity and ubiquity.

**Key claims**:
- Mental characteristics cannot plausibly emerge from dead matter without precedent.
- Proto-mental properties at fundamental level avoid arbitrary discontinuity.
- We may never have "conception of the physical" adequate to exclude mentality.

**Limitations**: Brief essay; does not address combination problem in detail.

**Program use**: Argument from Continuity (A2); historical anchor.

---

### Strawson, G. (2006). "Realistic Monism: Why Physicalism Entails Panpsychism." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 13(10–11), 3–31.

**Relevance**: Central. Russellian monism → panpsychism entailment.

**Key claims**:
- Real physicalism requires everything concrete to be physical.
- Physicalism is "realistic" only if physical stuff is experiential (or proto-experiential) in intrinsic nature.
- "Realistic physicalism" entails panpsychism or near variant.

**Limitations**: Controversial entailment claim; critics deny physicalism requires experiential intrinsics.

**Program use**: A1, A5; Russellian monism foundation.

---

### James, W. (1890). *The Principles of Psychology*, Vol. 1. Henry Holt.

**Relevance**: Moderate. Continuity and "mind-dust" combination problem origin.

**Key claims**:
- Consciousness may exist in minimal form throughout nature ("mind-dust").
- Combination problem: how do parts compose unified experience?

**Limitations**: Pre-quantum, pre-IIT; speculative neuroscience.

**Program use**: Historical combination problem; A2 continuity precedent.

---

### Russell, B. (1927). *The Analysis of Matter*. Routledge.

**Relevance**: Central. Structural vs. intrinsic nature distinction.

**Key claims**:
- Physics describes causal/structural properties only.
- Intrinsic nature of matter unknown; neutral monism possible.

**Limitations**: Does not specifically endorse panpsychism; neutral monism variant.

**Program use**: A1 Intrinsicality; Russellian monism label.

---

## 2. Contemporary Panpsychist Defense

### Goff, P. (2019). *Galileo's Error: Foundations for a New Science of Consciousness*. Pantheon.

**Relevance**: Central. Public and academic synthesis.

**Key claims**:
- Galileo restricted science to quantitative properties, excluding consciousness.
- Science tells how things behave, not what they are (Russellian).
- Panpsychism is simplest response to hard problem.
- Combination via "subject-summing" and fusion variants.

**Limitations**: Popular presentation; some claims oversimplified; cosmopsychism flirtation.

**Program use**: Primary contemporary defense; combination solutions; outreach framing.

---

### Goff, P., Seager, W., & Allen-Hermanson, S. (2022). Panpsychism. *Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy*.

**Relevance**: High. Authoritative overview.

**Key claims**: Taxonomy of panpsychism (constitutive, emergent, compositional); combination problem variants; relation to physicalism.

**Program use**: Variant selection (Russellian panprotopsychism); objection catalog.

---

### Seager, W. (1995). *Theories of Consciousness*. Routledge. (See also 2016 *Panpsychism* entry in Routledge Encyclopedia.)

**Relevance**: High. Fusion model for combination.

**Key claims**:
- Fusion: micro-subjects combine into new subject, not mere aggregation.
- Panpsychism as research program, not dogma.

**Limitations**: Fusion metaphysics obscure; empirical hooks limited.

**Program use**: A4 composition; combination_model.py fusion option.

---

### Brüntrup, G., & Jaskolla, L. (Eds.) (2016). *Panpsychism: Contemporary Perspectives*. Oxford University Press.

**Relevance**: High. Anthology of current debates.

**Key claims**: Multiple panpsychist and anti-panpsychist positions; cosmopsychism; Russellian monism; combination.

**Program use**: Objection matrix sources; cosmopsychism fallback.

---

### Chalmers, D. (2013). "Panpsychism and Panprotopsychism." In Brüntrup & Jaskolla (Eds.).

**Relevance**: Central. Panprotopsychism formulation.

**Key claims**:
- Panprotopsychism: fundamental entities have proto-experiential properties, not full mentality.
- Cosmopsychism as combination solution.
- Priority of micro vs. macro contested.

**Program use**: Primary variant (panprotopsychism); A3 refinement.

## 3. Objections and Alternatives

### Coleman, S. (2014). "The Real Combination Problem: Panpsychism, Micro-Subjects, and Emergence." *Erkenntnis*, 79, 19–44.

**Relevance**: High. Combination problem sharpened.

**Key claims**:
- Even if micro-experiences exist, explaining macro-subject unity is distinct problem.
- "Real" combination problem harder than often assumed.

**Program use**: objection_responses.md — combination; motivates IIT/fusion/cosmopsychism.

---

### Churchland, P. M. (1996). "The Rediscovery of Light." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 3(5–6), 358–368.

**Relevance**: Moderate. Eliminativist contrast.

**Key claims**: Qualia may be eliminated with neuroscience progress; hard problem dissolves.

**Program use**: Alternative to defeat; G4 physicalism path.

---

### Dennett, D. (1991). *Consciousness Explained*. Little, Brown.

**Relevance**: Moderate. Heterophenomenology; no qualia as usually conceived.

**Key claims**: Consciousness is cognitive access and self-model; "qualia" are theoretical posits.

**Program use**: Contrast with panpsychist realism about phenomenology.

---

### Frankish, K. (2016). "Illusionism as a Theory of Consciousness." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 23(11–12), 11–39.

**Relevance**: High. Primary modern illusionist challenge.

**Key claims**:
- Phenomenal properties are introspective illusions; no real qualia.
- Science should explain why we *think* we have qualia.

**Program use**: G1 falsifier; strongest empirical competitor if successful.

---

### Searle, J. (1992). *The Rediscovery of the Mind*. MIT Press.

**Relevance**: Moderate. Biological naturalism.

**Key claims**: Consciousness is biological phenomenon caused by brain processes; not substrate-neutral.

**Program use**: P5 falsifier; biological chauvinism opponent.

---

## 4. Empirical Bridges: IIT and Neuroscience

### Tononi, G. (2004). "An Information Integration Theory of Consciousness." *BMC Neuroscience*, 5, 42.

**Relevance**: Central. Formal integration measure.

**Key claims**:
- Consciousness = integrated information (Φ).
- System is conscious to extent its information is unified and irreducible.

**Limitations**: Φ computation intractable for large systems; postulates revised (IIT 3.0, 4.0).

**Program use**: P1, P4, P5; iit_meta_analysis.py.

---

### Oizumi, M., Albantakis, L., & Tononi, G. (2014). "From the Phenomenology to the Mechanisms of Consciousness: Integrated Information Theory 3.0." *PLoS Computational Biology*, 10(5), e1003588.

**Relevance**: Central. IIT 3.0 axioms and Φ.

**Key claims**: Five axioms of experience → postulates of physical mechanisms; exclusion principle.

**Program use**: P1 formalism; combination as integration.

---

### Koch, C., & Tononi, G. (2017). "Can Machines Be Conscious?" *IEEE Spectrum*.

**Relevance**: High. Substrate neutrality implications.

**Key claims**: IIT implies some machines may be conscious if Φ sufficient; ethical implications.

**Program use**: P5; cross-substrate predictions.

---

### Massimini, M., et al. (2005). "Breakdown of Cortical Effective Connectivity During Sleep." *Science*, 309(5744), 2228–2232.

**Relevance**: High. Integration loss during unconsciousness.

**Key claims**: Effective connectivity breaks down in sleep; correlates with loss of consciousness.

**Program use**: P4 anesthesia/sleep; anesthesia_review.md.

### Barrett, A. B., & Mediano, P. A. (2019). "Phi Is Not Consciousness." *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 42, e281.

**Relevance**: High. Critical challenge to P1.

**Key claims**: Φ can be high in systems intuitively non-conscious; Φ ≠ consciousness without further constraints.

**Program use**: P1-F3; evidence_ledger contradicting studies.

## 10. Gaps in Literature (Research Opportunities)

1. **Direct panpsychist experimental protocol** — No standard lab test for proto-experience; IIT is proxy.
2. **Combination formalism** — IIT addresses integration, not subject unity metaphysics.
3. **Cross-substrate organoid/AI Φ** — P5 data scarce.
4. **Psychedelic Φ during experience** — P6 needs simultaneous subjective report + integration metrics.
5. **Russellian monism and physics** — Intrinsic nature underdetermined by current physics.

These gaps define Tier 2–3 experimental agenda in `predictions.md`.
