## 1. Hyperscanning and Inter-Brain Synchrony

### Method overview

**Hyperscanning**: simultaneous recording (EEG, fMRI, fNIRS) from two or more interacting brains.

**Key metrics:**
- Inter-brain phase locking value (PLV)
- Inter-brain coherence
- Transfer entropy / Granger causality across brains
- Dual-brain network analysis

### Hasson et al. (2012). *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* — speaker-listener coupling

**Findings**: Listener brain activity mirrors speaker with delay; coupling predicts comprehension.

**BFC relevance**: Baseline for **social coupling**; not full "we-subject" but cross-brain information flow.

### Jiang et al. (2015). *Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience* — cooperation vs competition

**Findings**: Inter-brain synchrony higher during cooperative vs competitive tasks.

**P7 support**: Coupling increases with **joint intent**, not passive co-presence.

### Sänger et al. (2012). *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* — guitar duets

**Findings**: Phase synchronization between guitarists' brains during coordinated play; leader-follower asymmetry.

**P7 support**: **Joint improvisation > passive** pattern begins; music as collective flow laboratory.

### Yun et al. (2012). *NeuroImage* — inter-brain coherence in cooperation

**Findings**: Increased inter-brain beta-band coherence during cooperative button-press tasks.

**P7 support**: Structured cooperation elevates coupling.

## 2. Ritual, Choral Performance, and Collective Effervescence

### Durkheim (1912). *The Elementary Forms of Religious Life*

**Concept**: Collective effervescence — ritual generates temporary social unity, emotional arousal, boundary dissolution between self and group.

**BFC relevance**: Phenomenological precursor to **collective re-coupling (F4)**; not neural data but motivates P7 predictions.

### Pearce et al. (2015). *Scientific Reports* — religious ritual and pain tolerance

**Findings**: Rowdy ritual participants show higher pain tolerance; synchronized arousal in group context.

**BFC relevance**: Behavioral collective state; neural hyperscanning limited.

### Kleinspehn-Ammerlahn et al. (2011). *Consciousness and Cognition* — choral singing

**Findings**: Choir singers report unity, absorption; EEG shows altered states during group performance.

**P7 hook**: Choral performance as Tier 3 protocol candidate.

## 3. Shared Psychotic Disorder and Mass Delusion

### Shared psychotic disorder (DSM: delusional disorder by association)

**Phenomenology**: Inducer transmits delusional system to close associate(s); shared reality tunnel.

**BFC reading (speculative):**
- **Pathological coupling** — abnormally high belief synchrony
- **Filter failure (F5)** — boundary between subjects thins without organized integration
- Distinct from **organized group flow** (P8 prediction)

### Wehmeier et al. (2003). *Canadian Journal of Psychiatry* — folie à deux review

**Findings**: Dyads with intense emotional bonds; social isolation common.

**P8 relevance**: Case for cross-subject **narrative binding** without IIT/PCI data—gap for future study.

### Mass psychogenic illness / social contagion

**Examples**: Dancing plagues, conversion outbreaks, online radicalization cascades.

**BFC relevance**: Large-N **collective coupling** at behavioral/social level; neural hyperscanning absent.

**Caution**: Sociological/psychiatric explanations dominate; do not over-metaphysicalize.

## 4. Class Consciousness and Mass Movements

### Marx — class consciousness (structural, not phenomenal)

**BFC creative reading**: Mass strikes, revolutions describe **"we became one"** phenomenology in participants.

**Empirical**: Autonomic synchrony at protests; narrative synchrony on social media; limited inter-brain data.

**Program stance**: Metaphorically informative; not primary evidence base.

## 5. P7 vs P8: Organized Fusion vs Filter Failure

| Dimension | P7 Organized coupling (ritual, flow) | P8 Filter failure (psychosis, some psychedelic) |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Global connectivity | Structured increase | Often increased but disorganized |
| PCI / integration | Higher or maintained | Lower relative to richness |
| Subjective report | "We," unity, flow | Over-connection, cosmic significance, confusion |
| Cross-brain coupling | High + organized | May be high + chaotic |
| BFC mode | `COLLECTIVE_COUPLING` | `FILTER_FAILURE` |

**Key falsifiable split (P8):** If psychosis and choral singing show **identical** connectivity profiles, BFC organized/failure distinction fails.

## 6. Tier 3 Protocol (from plan)

**Hyperscanning collective flow study (P7):**

1. Dyads/triads: baseline conversation vs joint improvisation vs passive co-viewing
2. Metrics: inter-brain PLV, transfer entropy, dual-EEG PCI-analog
3. Subjective: we-ness scales (Inclusion of Other in Self), micro-phenomenological interviews
4. Controls: shuffled dyads, matched stimulus without interaction
5. **BFC prediction**: joint improvisation > conversation > passive for irreducible coupling

**Filter failure comparison (P8):**

Compare literature bins: psychedelic (organized connectivity increase per Petri 2014), schizophrenia (disorganized connectivity in some studies), structured ritual (organized cross-brain coupling)—map to entropy–integration space.

## 8. Research Gaps

1. **Irreducibility test**: few studies use shuffled-dyad controls for inter-brain metrics
2. **PCI during group flow**: not standard in hyperscanning literature
3. **Shared psychosis + EEG**: ethically difficult; case reports only
4. **We-ness ↔ coupling correlation**: under-studied with micro-phenomenology
