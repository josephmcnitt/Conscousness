# Creativity and Consciousness: Literature Review

Desk synthesis for **P10–P12** (Field Excitation Ontology / F8).

---

## Flow

### Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1990). *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience*. Harper.

**Key claims:** Optimal experience when challenge matches skill; loss of self-consciousness; autotelic absorption.

**FEO mapping:** Excitation at stability edge; moderate filter opening.

**P10 relevance:** Predicts distinct integration profile vs boredom and anxiety.

### Dietrich, A. (2004). Neurocognitive mechanisms underlying the experience of flow. *Consciousness and Cognition*.

**Key claims:** Transient hypofrontality during flow; reduced explicit monitoring.

**FEO mapping:** Reduced top-down filter, not filter failure.

---

## Insight

### Kounios & Beeman (2014). The cognitive neuroscience of insight. *Annual Review of Psychology*.

**Key claims:** Insight involves sudden reorganization; right hemisphere anterior superior temporal gyrus; preparation and incubation phases.

**P11 relevance:** Pre-insight neural reorganization supports mode transition hypothesis.

### Sandkühler & Bhattacharya (2008). Deconstructing insight. *PLoS ONE*.

**Key claims:** EEG alpha power changes precede insight solutions.

**P11 hook:** Measurable pre-insight signatures exist—test Φ analog.

---

## Improvisation and Jazz

### Limb & Braun (2008). Neural substrates of spontaneous musical performance. *PLoS ONE*.

**Key claims:** Jazz improvisation: decreased lateral PFC, increased medial PFC; self-expression network.

**P12 relevance:** Creative improv has distinct neural signature vs memorized performance.

### Donnay et al. (2014). Interaction of improvisation and topology in jazz musicians' brain networks. *NeuroImage*.

**Key claims:** Network topology changes during improvisation.

**P12 hook:** Creative joint performance ≠ routine motor task.

---

## Default Mode and Creativity

### Beaty et al. (2016). Creative cognition and brain network dynamics. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*.

**Key claims:** Creativity involves default mode + executive networks; dynamic coupling.

**FEO mapping:** Mode exploration requires coordinated substructures within excitation.

---

## Psychedelics and REBUS

### Carhart-Harris et al. (2014). The entropic brain. *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*.

**Key claims:** Psychedelics increase entropy; relax high-level priors (REBUS).

**FEO mapping:** Flattened mode landscape—creative or psychotic depending on integration (P8).

See also [`psychedelic_reanalysis.md`](psychedelic_reanalysis.md).
